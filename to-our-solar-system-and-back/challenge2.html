Now that you have selected your celestial object, you need to collect samples and bring them back to Earth. First, let's explore the four factors affecting flight and landing: thrust, lift, weight, and drag.
Select the terms below to learn more:
THRUST
Thrust is the upward motion force that propels the rocket upward and off the planet's surface. It is a reaction to the force of the fuel combustion occurring in the rocket engines.
LIFT & WEIGHT
If a rocket launch is successful, lift is the propelling force that uses thrust to overcome the weight (the force pulling down the mass) of the rocket.
DRAG
Drag is another opposing force to weight and is produced by the forces of resistance and friction, including air resistance. Drag and friction would not be found in a vacuum, like space!

Materials Scientist
A materials scientist uses chemistry, biology, and engineering to discover new materials.
Aerospace Engineer and Operations Technician
Aerospace engineers and operations technicians operate and maintain equipment used in developing, testing, producing, and sustaining new aircraft and spacecraft.
Chemist
Chemists and materials scientists study substances at the atomic and molecular levels and analyze the ways in which the substances interact with one another. They use their knowledge to develop new and improved products.
Electrician
Electricians install, maintain, and repair electrical power, communications, lighting, and control systems in homes, businesses, and factories - and spacecraft!
Geoscientist
Geoscientists study the physical aspects of Earth, such as its composition, structure, and processes, to learn about its past, present, and future.
