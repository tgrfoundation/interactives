Natural hazards become disasters when people's lives and livelihoods are destroyed. Sometimes it can be difficult to reach people in need during or right after a natural disaster. Robots can help us assess damage and send back important information to aid in rescue efforts.
Click on each item below to learn about the equipment used on rescue robots.
Wheels
Circular objects that revolve on an axle and are fixed below the robot to enable it to move easily over the ground.
Wings
Horizontal structures that project from both sides of a robot to support it in the air.
Legs
Limbs on which a robot can walk and stand.
First Aid
A collection of supplies and equipment that is used to give medical treatment.
Floatation Device
A piece of equipment designed to assist the wearer to keep afloat in water.
Temperature Sensor
A sensor that can read the temperature of an object or environment.
Liquid Depth Sensor
A sensor that measures water pressure to indicate depth underwater.
Touch Sensor
A sensor that reacts when touch is applied, usually in the form of a button.
Ultrasonic Sensor
A sensor that uses high-frequency sound waves like a bat to measure the distance to an object.
Underwater Propeller
An underwater thruster that is a configuration of marine propellers and hydraulic or electric motor built into, or mounted to a robot.
Gyro Sensor
A sensor that reacts to changes in motion or orientation.
Infrared Cameras
A camera that detects infrared wavelengths — invisible to the human eye — to register different temperature levels.
