An animal's internal anatomy can also provide clues as to how it may be related to other animals. However, an animal's anatomy does not always suggest how closely different animals may be related, which is why this room is just one of your stops before you make a final decision.
You don't need a science lab to see what an animal looks like on the outside.

Underneath the layers of fur or feathers and skin exists a skeleton unique to each species.

What can we learn from an animal's skeleton? How can it help us further investigate how animals are related?

As you can see, these humeri are homologous — or similar. Similarities often occur in animals when they have a common ancestor.

However, their anatomy also remains slightly different because each animal adapted over time to survive best in its own environment.
